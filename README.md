# Career Interviewer Skill
**by [Delma Brocales](https://itsdelma.com) · made with Claude**

---

Most people can't tell their own career story clearly. Not because they haven't done interesting work, but because they're too close to it. They undersell the hard things, they say "we" when they mean "I," and they've never had someone outside their own head reflect back what's actually there.

This is a Claude skill that does something about that.

---

## What it does

It's a conversational career interviewer. It walks you through your career phase by phase, asks follow-up questions when something deserves more depth, and produces two distinct outputs at the end.

**Output 1 — Your Narrative Timeline**
A long-form, story-driven record of your career experience. Not a résumé. Not a list of jobs. The actual story, written in prose, with the real texture of what you've built. Something you could share with a hiring manager, use as source material for a portfolio, or hand to anyone who wants to understand what you've done and how you think.

**Output 2 — Your Private Coach Assessment**
This one is just for you. It's what an outside eye actually sees when it reads your story — the patterns you've been living but never named, the strengths you've been describing as "just how I work," and the places where your self-narrative and the evidence don't quite match.

This is the part most people find most valuable. It names your invisible thesis — the thing you've been doing your whole career without ever having a single sentence for it. It tells you, directly and kindly, what you're not seeing about yourself.

There's an optional third step where the skill offers a few high-level angles for how you might frame your story going forward — but only after the first two.

---

## What makes it different

- It interviews you like a journalist, not a form
- It catches when you undersell yourself and gently pushes back
- It writes your timeline as narrative prose — no bullet points, no LinkedIn-speak
- The coach assessment is honest in the way a trusted colleague would be: direct, warm, and not interested in making you feel good at the expense of telling you something true
- It adapts to where you are in your career — early, mid, or senior — without asking

## How to install

**On claude.ai (Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise):**

1. Download the `career-interviewer.skill` file from this repo
2. In Claude, go to **Settings → Capabilities** and make sure **Code Execution and File Creation** is turned on
3. Go to **Settings → Customize → Skills** and upload the file
4. Toggle it on
5. Open a new conversation and tell Claude you want to document your career story — it'll take it from there

**On Claude Code:**

Drop the `career-interviewer` folder into `~/.claude/skills/` and you're ready to go.

---

## How to use it

Start a new conversation and say something like:

- *"I want to document my career story"*
- *"Help me tell my career story"*
- *"Interview me about my work history"*
- *"I don't know how to talk about my experience"*

The skill will introduce itself and walk you through the rest. Plan for 30–60 minutes depending on how many chapters your career has.

---

## Who this is for

Anyone who has done real work and struggles to talk about it clearly. Designers, engineers, writers, operators, founders, people returning from sabbaticals, people who've been laid off, people preparing for interviews, people building a portfolio, people who just want to understand what their career actually adds up to.

You don't need to be at a certain level or in a certain field. You just need a career worth documenting and some honesty about how it actually went.
